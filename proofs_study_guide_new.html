Partition
Definition: A partition of set \(S\) is a collection \(\{A_i\}_{i \in I}\) such that:
  1. \(A_i \neq \emptyset\) for all \(i\) (each part is non-empty)
  2. \(A_i \cap A_j = \emptyset\) for \(i \neq j\) (parts are pairwise disjoint)
  3. \(\bigcup_{i \in I} A_i = S\) (parts cover all of \(S\))

Peano Axioms
The natural numbers \(\mathbb{N}\) satisfy:
  1. \(1 \in \mathbb{N}\) (1 is a natural number)
  2. If \(n \in \mathbb{N}\), then \(S(n) \in \mathbb{N}\) (successor exists)
  3. \(S(n) \neq 1\) for all \(n\) (1 is not a successor)
  4. \(S(n) = S(m) \Rightarrow n = m\) (successor is injective)
  5. If \(K \subseteq \mathbb{N}\), \(1 \in K\), and \(n \in K \Rightarrow S(n) \in K\), then \(K = \mathbb{N}\) (induction)

  • Start: You have a "1" block
  • Successor: You can always add "+1" to get the next number
  • No going back: You can never get back to 1 by adding
  • No collisions: Different numbers have different successors
  • That's all: Every natural number comes from starting at 1 and adding
